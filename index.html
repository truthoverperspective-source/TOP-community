Latest Developments: Trauma

Curated, easy-to-read summaries of the newest peer-reviewed trauma research.

Effects of Early Intervention on PTSD Symptoms in Veterans

A new study published in the Journal of Traumatic Stress found that veterans who received trauma-focused therapy within the first 30 days after a traumatic event showed significantly lower PTSD symptom severity at 6-month follow-up.

The research highlighted the importance of rapid access to care and suggested that early intervention may prevent the chronicity of trauma responses.

These findings support expanding same-week therapy options for first responders and military personnel.

PubMed • July 9, 2026

EMDR vs. Prolonged Exposure: Long-term Outcomes in Complex Trauma

Researchers compared Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) with Prolonged Exposure therapy in patients with complex trauma histories.

Both treatments were effective, but EMDR showed faster symptom reduction and better retention rates.

The study suggests tailoring treatment choice based on patient preference and dissociation levels.

PubMed • July 8, 2026

The Role of Sleep Quality in Trauma Recovery

Poor sleep in the weeks following trauma was associated with higher risk of developing chronic PTSD according to a large longitudinal study.

Interventions targeting sleep hygiene alongside therapy showed promising results in improving overall recovery.

Clinicians are encouraged to screen for and address sleep disturbances early in treatment.
